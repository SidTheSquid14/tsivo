Nouns

A person, place, thing, or idea.

Example

Look at that cool CAR


Verb

Expresses action or a state of being.

Example

He EATS his chicken biryani


Adjectives

A word or phrase naming an attribute, added to or grammatically related to a noun to modify or describe it.

Example

That BAD game is called Fortnite


Prepositions

a word governing, and usually preceding, a noun or pronoun and expressing a relation to another word or element in the clause

Example

Why is there a car ON that roof?


Pronouns

A pronoun (I, me, he, she, herself, you, it, that, they, each, few, many, who, whoever, whose, someone, everybody, etc.) is a word that takes the place of a noun.

Example

THEY are best friends.


Conjunctions

A conjunction is a part of speech that connects words, phrases, or clauses. Remember FANBOYS - For, And, Nor, But, Or, Yet, So

Example

I wanted to go to the soccer game, but my mom said that I had to finish my homework first.


Interjections

An interjection is a part of speech that demonstrates the emotion or feeling of the author. These words or phrases can stand alone, or be placed before or after a sentence.

Example

WOW, what a goal!


Adverbs

A word or phrase that modifies or qualifies an adjective, verb, or other adverb or a word group, expressing a relation of place, time, circumstance, manner, cause, degree, etc.

Example

That car is QUICKLY accelerating.
